ComFree-Sim: A GPU-Parallel Analytical Contact Physics Engine for Accelerating Contact-Rich Robotics Simulation and Control

Chetan Borse1,     Zhixian Xie1,     Wei-Cheng Huang2,    Wanxin Jin1
1Arizona State University    2University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign
Preprint Code Repo Video demo


Overview: ComFree-Sim is a GPU-parallel analytical contact physics engine that utilizes complementarity-free contact modeling and achieves 2-3 times higher throughput than MJWarp in dense contact scenarios while maintaining comparable physical fidelity. The system's low-latency performance significantly enhances the success rates of high-frequency controllers like MPPI for dexterous manipulation and reduces the computational overhead of dynamics-aware retargeting.



How does ComFree-Sim work?


ComFree-Sim has two key components.
1. Complementarity-free contact modeling
2. GPU implementation for contact resolution


More Results


Real Time MPC for Dexterous Manipulation

In object reorientation tasks, the LEAP Hand rotates the cube to the desired pose. We implement MPPI with ComFree-Sim and achieve real-time control at ~35-72Hz. We deploy the system for 4 objects.


  • Cube
  • Duck
  • Spam Tin
  • Cylinder

Dynamics-Aware Retargeting For Agile Locomotion

We also demonstrate the ContactSDF model on a Allegro hand for on-palm object reorientation. The system includes a 6-DoF object laying on the palm of the Allegro hand and the fingers are actuated by a low-level joint PD controller. The goal is to reorient the object (solid) from an initial pose to a desired target pose (transparent). Click the tabs to check the manipulation results for different objects.
